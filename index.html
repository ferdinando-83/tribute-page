Marcian Edward "Ted" Hoff Jr.

Co-Inventor of the Microprocessor

Historical Timeline:


  • Born October 28th, 1937 in Rochester, New York
  • Received a bachelor's degree in electrical engineering from the Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute in 1958
  • Received a National Science Foundation Fellowship to enroll in Stanford University, where he received his master's degree in 1959
  • Earned his Ph.D. in 1962 and as part of his Ph.D. dissertation, Hoff co-invented the least mean squares filter with Bernard Widrow
  • Joined Intel in 1968 as employee number 12, and is credited with coming up with the idea of using a "universal processor"
  • Assisted with applying the universal processor idea with Stanley Mazor in 1969 to the Intel 4004â€”the chip that started the microprocessor revolution in the early 1970s
  • In 1980, Hoff was named the first Intel Fellow, which is the highest technical position in the company
  • He stayed in that position until 1983 when he left for Atari and remained with Atari until March 1995
  • In 1996 he became executive vice president of sales and marketing for Sega of America
